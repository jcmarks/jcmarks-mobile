Accountability: About This Data Set

Personal data collection: How awareness of my activities and the study impact accountability.

Where did these data come from?

The usage data used for this analysis were collected from 30 Jan to 19 Feb using AWARE on my personal phone. Knowledge of my phone use patterns and activities make me think that the data collected are an accurate representation of my phone usage. I had my phone with me each day of this study, and the phone's battery was never completely dead, despite the necesity to charge it more through the study duration.

Who has access to these data?

Only I have access to the raw data, as my database is password protected.

About the data analysis

I decided to ask a question about device usage in an attempt to ground my study in something against which I could measure it. When I first looked at my data, I found myself trying to use the data to fit activities that I thought I was engaging in. Without the use of a metric against which to measure my data usage, I could interpret my data however I pleased. Earlier in the semester, we had read the ProactiveTasks paper, and I was intrigued by the various patterns that were determined about people. I wanted to use the ProactiveTasks paper as a framework on which to base my own analysis.
