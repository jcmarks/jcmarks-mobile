Quality: About This Data Set

Personal Data Collection: The promises and woes of collecting mobile data

Are the data complete?

The phone usage data do seem to be complete. Looking at my data, I plotted activity as a function of time for each day to ensure that there were data throughout the day. Any breaks in the data were a result of times when it made sense for me not to be using my phone: during class times, customer meetings, and during the night (although I was sad to see that the time my phone was off at night was less than I had hoped).

An in-process step I took to ensure completeness was to charge my phone each night, and to be sure to carry it with me. Typically, I only charge my phone every few days, but due to AWARE, a daily charge was required to be sure that my usage data would be complete.

Are the data coherent?

I performed several checks on my data through the course of the analysis to make sure that they were coherent. The first check was the data usage as a function of time: was phone usage being logged during the times when I would be using my phone? As discussed above, the only gaps that I saw in the data were accountable by the times I was asleep, so the times seemed to be coherent. An additional confirmation of coherence is the fact that the distributions on different days looked similar.

The next coherence check I performed was the total amount of time logged: 30 hours in approximately three weeks. Again, this made some intuitive sense to me. I regularly check and respond to emails on my phone, use Skype, and have been using the stopwatch feature to time runs. Although a bit higher that I would like to report (considering productivity arguments), I find this amount of time believable.

I finally performed a coherence check on actual times being recorded. At a particular point in time, I looked at my phone for a duration of approximately 10 seconds. Having marked that time, I could locate the usage time in the raw data, and confirmed that it was approximately 10 seconds in duration. This gave me confidence that each duration being recorded was coherent.

Are the data correct?

As mentioned, I performend a cross-check of phone screen on time - I used my phone for a set duration at a set time and then checked in the raw data file to ensure that the measured duration was the same as I had predicted.
